\noindent \underline{\textit{Turning point}} If we examine the criteria for a turning car to be clear of obstacles, we can model our FORWARD avoidance protocols after this case. It is known that to turn, the driver of a car should not turn the steering wheel until the shoulder of the car is past the outermost point of the obstacle. In a similar vein, we can assume the user of FORWARD follows the walker from behind. Therefore, if the walker itself is past the obstacle, the user should be successfully guided past (in a turn maneuver) without bumping.\\
